8.5: Dangerous Goods (DG)
Dangerous goods are solids, liquids, or gases that can harm people, other living organisms, property, or the environment. They require specialized methods of handling, transporting, storing, and disposing. They are often subject to both local and international regulations. Dangerous goods include materials that are radioactive, flammable, explosive, corrosive, oxidizing, asphyxiating, biohazardous, toxic, pathogenic, or allergenic.
Classification of DG:
PSA group:
A classification by PSA Chemcare to further assess the extent of hazard within each DG so as to determine the appropriate handling and storage rules within PSA.
DG are identified, handled and stored according to the following classification criteria:
  1. IMO Class: A number (1-9), designated by IMO to identify the hazard type of each DG.
  2. UN Number: A 4-digit ID used to identify major chemicals as well as generic chemical groups.
