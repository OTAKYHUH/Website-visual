Competency Assessment Checklist for YAS

At the end of the month of OJT, a mentor will be assigned to conduct the competency assessment to determine the current standing of the trainee. The mentor is responsible for assessing the trainee on the individual tasks and filling in the performance standards between a scale of 0 – 4.

1 – Need more practice, 2 – Pass, 3 – Good, 4 – Excellent

Task Outcome Performance Standard Mentor’s Comments
1 2 3 4
PERFORMANCE ASSESSMENT
1. Use of Systems
CITOS / Logical Yard / Yard QCC
  • Navigate block view, enhanced activity, and micro plan view mode.
  • Replan successfully by cluster or block.
  • Identify container details correctly.
VOMC
  • Understand vessel sequence and panel (VPA).
  • Differentiate view vs edit mode.
  • Recognize colour codes for operations.
YOMC
  • Use YOMC, Diversion Panel, and PM monitoring effectively.
  • Handle diversions and DG diversions correctly.
  • Monitor PM productivity and no-shows.
2. Yard Layout & Equipment
  • Match crane types to yard zones.
  • Understand yard characteristics.
Special Areas (Door Turn, Bund, Jammed Cone)
  • Locate door turn, bund, and jammed cone areas.
  • Apply safety procedures.
  • Troubleshoot jammed equipment.
Layout (P1–3)
  • Read yard maps and locate containers.
  • Apply segregation rules (DG, reefer, etc.).
Contact List
  • Identify correct escalation channels.
  • Demonstrate correct contact usage in a scenario.
3. Monitor Yard Operations & PM Optimization
Monitoring
  • Track no-shows.
  • Analyze PM waiting times.
  • Use reports for optimization.
4. Communication & Coordination
Radioset Communication
  • Apply correct radioset numbering format.
  • Accurately identify equipment via radioset numbers.
Email Communication
  • Draft timely, clear emails highlighting yard congestion, blocks affected, and mitigation actions.
5. Activity Management
Shifting & Replanning
  • Suspend non-urgent activities during peak.
  • Coordinate meal breaks during lull.
  • Apply kill-block strategy.
  • Raise ITT boxes as needed.
6. Handle & Escalate Yard Exceptions
Exception Handling
  • Identify missing containers, PM breakdowns, leaks, uncontactable PM, etc.
  • Escalate as per protocol (YODM, SRDO, CSO, etc.).
  • Document exceptions clearly.
SUPERVISORY SKILLS ASSESSMENT
7. Manage Resources
Knows the strategies and guidelines to achieve KPI targets
8. Solve Problems and Make Decisions
Identify relevant issues and causes of problems
Make effective and practical decisions
Show firmness and impartiality during course of work
Explain the rationale behind work processes
9. Manage and Guide Subordinates
Provide guidance and corrections to CES & AES at work
ATTITUDE ASSESSMENT
10. Provide Customer Service
Answer to customer queries and able to direct to proper personnel involved
Phone etiquette handling customers
11. Manage Job Performance
Conscientious in execution of duties and responsibilities
Willing and able to change his way of working when required
Willing to perform more than what is required of his assignment and job without being told to do so
