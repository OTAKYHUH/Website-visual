Introduction

The container terminal operates round-the-clock and the Automated Yard Duty Manager (aYDM) manages the terminal yard operations during shift. The Replanner (RP) assist the aYDM by ensuring that yard operations within section are carried out smoothly, efficiently and safely whilst meeting Operational targets. Important RP targets include ensuring good Prime Move (PM) productivity via good yard waiting time.

Objective

The objective of this OJT programme is to enable the RP to:

  • Familiarize himself with the roles and responsibilities of the RP
  • To learn and understand the skills required for him to function effectively and independently in his role as an RP
  • To be aware of the operational situations faced in the Container Terminal and how to make correct decisions to achieve the best yard performance possible

Tasks

During his training programme, the trainee will observe his mentor carrying out his RP duties, which can include the following:

  • Monitoring of yard service level and productivity
  • Monitoring and minimizing yard congestion (RTGs, ARMGs)
  • Handling common yard exceptions
  • Execute smooth shift changeover

With guidance from his Shift Mentor, he should use the guidelines / knowledge checklist to gauge the extent of his knowledge gained. RP should prepare himself to perform the role independently in the future by asking questions actively during OJT to better equip himself with the required skills.

The Section AM will review his assignments and assess his understanding using the Guidelines / Knowledge Checklist. The complete Task Book will be used as a record to determine if the trainee has attained the necessary competencies.

Task 6: Monitor Yard Operations

To optimize yard waiting time (the time where prime mover spends waiting in a yard during loading / discharging), several parameters can be considered.

  • By regularly monitoring no show report closely to identify high waiting PM (PC call checking on driver location)
  • By regularly monitoring the yard location panel and promptly assigning yard location, offloading waiting time can be significantly reduced
  • Immediately informing SOC possible to stagger or reduce PMs ratio if encounter heavy block mounting and working with AOC to put restricted region at the slot to focus on ship operation single point mounting job. Fully utilizing both ARMG lanes (lane 0 and lane 11) by changing PM lane if required.
  • Forecasted single point mounting slot, raising CMRs for ARMG to side shuffling loading container to other slot, these may utilize both ARMG crane for mounting when the job is assigned.
  • Regular replanning through Yard QCC and replanning single crane operation at the start of the shift.

  • Port Mark

Port Mark refers to designated discharging port of the vessel. Containers should not be stack if they are from different port mark within the same vessel.

Size

Size refers to the length of the container. Do not mix 20ft, 40ft and 45ft.

Category

Do not mix different types of container type. GP, HC, FE, FR, TK, OH/OW/HW and DG.

Weight

The lighter container should not be stacked on top of the heavier container. The weight indication for sea is in tonnage. : Dangerous Goods (DG)

Dangerous goods are solids, liquids, or gases that can harm people, other living organisms, property, or the environment. They require specialized methods of handling, transporting, storing, and disposing. They are often subject to both local and international regulations. Dangerous goods include materials that are radioactive, flammable, explosive, corrosive, oxidizing, asphyxiating, biohazardous, toxic, pathogenic, or allergenic.

Classification of DG:

PSA group:

A classification by PSA Chemcare to further assess the extent of hazard within each DG so as to determine the appropriate handling and storage rules within PSA.

DG are identified, handled and stored according to the following classification criteria:

  • IMO Class: A number (1-9), designated by IMO to identify the hazard type of each DG.
  • UN Number: A 4-digit ID used to identify major chemicals as well as generic chemical groups.
